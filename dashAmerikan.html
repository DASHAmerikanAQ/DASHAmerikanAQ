Fanfiction, Fan Response, and Adoption

×
Fanfiction is generally unpublished in a traditional sense, being primarily shared on the Internet, and before that in science fiction zines, however the bestselling Fifty Shades of Grey series was originally a piece of fanfiction. Karen Hellekson and Kristina Busse, The Fanfiction Studies Reader.

At several moments during the project we’ve inquired whether the kinds of conversations we've seen in the media presented by the personal and corporate entities of the Kardashians are conversations that are shared by their followers and fans. In order to identify and possibly understand conversational through-lines, the Karshashian's convergence culture, we thought we might look to some of the most engaged Kardashian fans—fanfiction writers. Since the late nineties, scholars have been studying the cultural phenomena of fan fiction, or the fannish practice of writing fiction using the characters and the worlds of beloved books, television shows, movies, and, etc.3 Fanfiction gives us even more ideas about the establishment of a Kardashian cultural community as well as the possible reification and rejection of particular discourses surrounding Kardashian celebrity. Or, what, if anything, are Kardashian fans producing in their position within the Kardashian media ecology?

The digital writing platform Wattpad provides a new community of young amateur writers to consider and interrogate. Unlike other fanfiction communities and archives, writers of Kardashian fanfiction produce most frequently on Wattpad. And it is interesting to note that these writers are young, generally teenaged. Unlike other popular fan fiction archives Wattpad operates on popularity metrics of "most viewed" and often publishes their popular author's finished work. The writers' choice of archive and its potential for monetization (Wattpad writer Anna Todd made a six-figure deal for her popular One Direction fanfiction in 2015) is particularly interesting when read through the lens of the Kardashians who always monetize the personal and the amateur. 4

In the tradition of fan fiction studies, we used fanfiction works, especially metadata, as a place to further interrogate the producer-consumer relations of the Kardashians and their audience. In order to begin sifting through this writing community, we built a scraping tool what collected metadata from stories that were tagged indicating they were about the Kardashians. Part of the tradition of fan fiction is a practice of generating effusive and specific tags and descriptions for stories. Thus, it seems potentially more interesting to see how authors not only are writing but also theorizing their own stories. What might we find in collective meta-engagement with individual works?

At first look, the themes within the canon of of Kardashian fanfiction seem to stick to two major tropes: a) the narrator finds she is a missing Kardashian sister and is rescued(!) from obscurity, or b) the narrator is adopted by a Kardashian or Jenner sister. The final scenario is decidedly a "group text" genre in which the writer is imagining what might happen in celebrity group chat. These stories generally feature Kylie and Kendall. It's notable that these narratives misalign with the trends of a majority of fanfiction, the romantic male-driven slash story. [add a link from dailydot]. In fact, without strong tragic narratives in need of "fixing" or male leads to transform into lovers, the Kardashian fandom manages to turn most fanfiction writing practices on their heads.

Wattpad Modelling (20 Topics)

When we turn to topic modeling of the descriptions we see these themes of adoption and communication solidify anew. At 20 topics, relationships, especially familial, present themselves throughout. These themes become especially apparent looking at topics 4, 5, 6, and 8 (which can be found in the modal window above). The topics of fame and family are interspersed throughout, and we see the alignment of Kardashian interest and response to it by fans. The same goes for topics of celebrity and their close ties to family or friendship. The concept of narrative ("story," "reading," "books," "writing") pops up across topics. We see the the influence of not only the act of writing, but also the ways in which the Kardashians fashion themselves as particular kinds of story-tellers (as reality tv personalities, producers of said show, and general content producers). Thus the themes of the show, family, legacy, matriarchy, are mostly reified through their writing fan base.

What we don't see in these fan interactions is the critique that often accompanies transformative fannish writing and fan response. We don't even see the mild interrogations of the tabloid writer or the sharp, quick-witted commentary of the twitter community. For these fanfiction writers, to keep up with the Kardashians is to love them, to insert themselves fully into their lives, to be recognized and accepted within the Kardashian matriarchal lineage.5
